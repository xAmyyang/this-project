Q2

Question:
How are discussions among committee members conducted? Are decisions made through formal voting, consensus, or led by a primary reviewer with input from others?

Justification:
Understanding how decisions are made is important for designing the system’s workflow. If decisions follow structured methods like voting or a lead reviewer, the system can be simpler. However, if multiple approaches are used, the system must support more complex collaboration.  
If not addressed, the system may not align with the actual decision-making process, leading to confusion or delays in finalising decisions. By addressing this, it ensures the system supports the correct workflow and enables smooth and efficient decision-making.

Question:
What happens when a reviewer becomes unavailable during the review process? Is the application reassigned, paused, or handled by the remaining members?

Justification:
This helps determine how the system should handle disruptions in the review process. The system must ensure continuity by supporting reassignment or workload balancing to avoid delays.  
If not addressed, this could block the reviewing pipeline when a reviewer becomes unavailable. By addressing this, it ensures the reviewing pipeline continues smoothly through reassignment or fallback mechanisms.
